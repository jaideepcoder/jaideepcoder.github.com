A lip-biting thriller with twists and turns you would never see coming. This is a story about a spy in the future but not that future we all see in the movies. It is a future we all are marching into, a future both grim and dark. Due to our own actions, the world is destabilizing, unsustainable. We might have succeeded and covered our problems with an aesthetic overcloak, but our problems are starting to show, now more than ever. The Energy Crisis. And it seems that the one person, who doesn't give a damn could be the only hope to stop it. But the world is bent on stopping him instead.

The year is 2026. Decades ago, the scientists had foretold that by the year 2026, our planets crude oil resources will not be able to support our growing populations requirements. And today... that prediction is becoming more than true. Its becoming a nightmare. Or it could be... but due to the research taking place in CERN under the guidance of world-renowned Dr. Elenore Basset, daughter of the Nobel laureate Dr. Giuseppe Beauvais, the world might get a viable alternate capable of sustaining the nine billion population of the planet.

The plot begins when a small paramilitary army attacks a remote underground lab in the SLHC complex in CERN with the primary objectives to kill every personnel involved in the project and destroy all their research data after making a secure copy of it. Alec Mercer, a security guard and Elenore Basset, the lead researcher of a secret project escape from their attackers after subduing them in the underground tunnels and managing to retrieve the research data from the mercenaries within the SLHC servers. Now, they find themselves being placed on the top of the terrorist lists and every covert actions and espionage agency is after them. But there's a catch. Alec Mercer is a CIA trained spy.
